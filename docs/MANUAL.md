# Lancet & Linen v1.1.0 — Manual & Lexicon

This manual is bilingual in the game (Deutsch/English). The sections below are
the maintained Markdown counterpart of the in-game **Manual & Lexicon** entry.
For sources and boundaries, see [HISTORICAL_SOURCES.md](HISTORICAL_SOURCES.md).

## 1. Core loop / Spielkern

Open the bathhouse, choose a patient, gather period-framed findings, and choose
hand-work or a next-day regimen. End the day to pay the household and advance
the calendar. Coin, stock, honour, civic standing and people in the household
all create trade-offs. Numbers are game abstractions, not historic price lists.

## 2. Roles / Rollen

The Bader combines elements that were distributed across local bath keepers,
barbers, wound workers and households. The game foregrounds the social stigma
of *Unehrlichkeit* without treating all towns or people as identical. A role is
a historical framing, not a claim about one universal profession.

## 3. Examinations / Untersuchungen

Inspection, pulse, urine viewing, touch and tongue viewing produce clues in the
language of humours and signs. They do not reveal modern causes. More findings
help the player make a legible game choice; they never create diagnostic
certainty. The Lepraschau is deliberately a civic judgement with consequences.

## 4. Procedures / Eingriffe

Procedures have supplies, requirements, risk and game outcomes. Bloodletting,
cupping, wound work and tooth work are historical motifs, not instructions.
The game does not claim effectiveness. A disabled action cannot be tapped,
triggered by hotkey, Enter, or focused keyboard activation; its reason remains
visible.

## 5. Regimens / Regimina

Regimens are deferred household plans: Pestregimen, rest and measured diet, or
bath and linen. They consume stock and resolve when the next day's doors open.
Their period framing includes air, rest, diet and linens; outcomes can be
steadier, unchanged or worse. Pestregimen is framed through miasma, never germ
theory, and is not medical advice.

## 6. Complaints / Beschwerden

Complaints are narrative game cases rather than diagnosis labels. Visible text
avoids later certainty for the legacy ulcer identifier. Birth assistance is a
rare, story-gated household referral; it is not a standard procedure. The
Lexicon records the evidence marker and source reference for every term.

## 7. Bathhouse / Badestube

Property brings daily cost as well as income. Staff roles provide apprenticeship
help, bathhouse demand, supply saving or sabotage resistance. Each person has a
trait — careful, sociable, thrifty or steadfast — whose concrete effect is
explained in the Staff screen. One staff event may occur at most every seven
game days and is journalled.

## 8. Town and craft / Stadt und Handwerk

Travel and markets are deliberately compressed. Nürnberg is mechanically
distinct: sworn craft, council and Rugsherren replace a generic guild-office
ladder. Other locations retain guild logic. Coin is a readable, simplified
Pfennig/Heller economy rather than a claim of stable cross-city purchasing
power.

## 9. Household and staff / Haushalt und Personal

A spouse may focus on household, trade or kinship. While together, household
reduces daily costs; trade slightly improves local purchase prices; kinship
contributes periodic civic access. Family, staff, prestige and affection feed
into later consequences rather than being decorative counters.

## 10. Politics and debt / Politik und Schuld

Offices require standing, elite reputation, council favour and coin. In
Nürnberg the guild-favour gate is replaced by civic oversight. Lombard debt
creates pressure. Repayment is active only when debt exists and the player has
at least one coin; otherwise its precise reason is shown below the disabled
control.

## 11. Act 3

Six saved consequence channels can fire once: epidemic memory, rival
reckoning, debt shadow, Lepraschau reputation, staff compact, and family
network. They apply only after their established conditions occur, write a
journal entry, and persist in the save. The ending therefore reads more of the
campaign than a final menu choice.

## 12. Sources and limits / Quellen und Grenzen

The Lexicon has at least 60 entries. Each has a German and English text,
category, evidence marker (`attested`, `regional`, `game simplification`) and a
source identifier. The binding bibliography and all explicit simplifications
are maintained in [HISTORICAL_SOURCES.md](HISTORICAL_SOURCES.md).
